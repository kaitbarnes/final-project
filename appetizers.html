Roasted Poblano Guacamole with Garlic and Parsley

Servings: Two and a half ounces of guacamole that feeds four to six people
Total time to cook: Around 15 minutes

Ingredients

  • Two medium (about six ounces) fresh poblano chiles
  • Six ounces ripe tomatoes
  • Two garlic cloves, unpeeled
  • Three tablespoons chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • Three medium-large (about 1 1/4 pounds total) ripe avocados
  • Salt
  • Two tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • Two tablespoons grated Mexican queso anejo or other dry grating cheese, such as Romano or Parmesan
  • A few slices of radish for garnish

Cooking Instructions

  1. The poblanos, tomatoes, and garlic: Lay the poblanos, tomatoes, and garlic on a baking sheet and set 4 inches below a very hot broiler. Roast, turning every couple of minutes, until the chiles and tomatoes are soft, blistered, and blackened in spots and the garlic is soft, 12 to 13 minutes. Place the chiles in a bowl, cover with a towel, and let stand for 5 minutes, then wipe off the blackened skin. Pull or cut out the stems, seed pods, and seeds; rinse quickly to remove any stray seeds and bits of char. When the tomatoes are cool, peel off and discard their skins. Slip the papery skins off the garlic. In a mortar or food processor, make a coarse puree of the roasted garlic and poblanos (with both mortar and processor, it's best to start with the garlic, then add the poblanos); place in a large bowl. Chop the roasted tomatoes (for this recipe, it's best not to use any of the juice from the baking sheet) and add to the poblano mixture along with the parsley.
  2. Finishing the guacamole: Cut the avocado lengthwise in half around the pit, twist the halves apart and remove the pits. Scoop out the flesh into the bowl with the flavorings. Using a potato masher or the back of a large spoon, coarsely mash everything together. Taste and season with salt, usually a scant teaspoon, then add enough lime juice to enliven all the flavors. Cover with plastic wrap, placing it directly on the surface, and refrigerate until you're ready to eat.
  3. To serve, scoop into a decorative bowl or Mexican mortar, sprinkle with the queso anejo and stud with radish slices.

Fried Ravioli Recipe

Servings: As much ravioli as you want
Total Time to Cook 10-15 minutes

Ingredients

  • Ravioli noodles
  • A cup of buttermilk
  • Bread crumbs or panko
  • Italian seasoning
Cooking Instructions
  1. Dip each ravioli noodle in buttermilk
  2. Cover each ravioli noodle in italian seasoned panko. Pro Tip: Wear gloves when doing this - it gets messy.
  3. Air fryer method - set air fryer to 350 degrees and cook for nine minutes.
  4. Deep fryer method - set deep fryer to 350 degrees and cook for three minutes.
  5. Once ravioli is golden brown, remove from fryer and let it stand for a minute. Add cheese to top of ravioli for extra treat. Serve with marinara sauce.
